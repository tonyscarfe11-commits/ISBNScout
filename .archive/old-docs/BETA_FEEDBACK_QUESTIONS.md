## Deep Dive Interview Questions

If you can get 3-5 beta testers on a 30-minute call, ask these:

### Background
1. Tell me about your book selling business
   - How long have you been selling?
   - Full-time or side hustle?
   - Where do you source books?
   - What platforms do you sell on?

2. Walk me through your current repricing process
   - How often do you check prices?
   - What tools do you use?
   - How long does it take?
   - What's frustrating about it?

### Usage
3. Show me how you used ISBNScout this week
   - [Screen share if possible]
   - What did you do first?
   - What worked well?
   - Where did you get stuck?

4. What were you trying to accomplish with ISBNScout?
   - Faster repricing?
   - Better prices?
   - More sales?
   - Save time?

### Value
5. Did ISBNScout solve your problem?
   - Yes: What changed?
   - No: What's missing?

6. How much time did it save you?
   - Per week?
   - Per book?
   - Worth paying for?

7. What would make this 10x more valuable to you?

### Pricing
8. How do you think about pricing for tools like this?
   - Per month?
   - Per listing?
   - One-time fee?
   - Percentage of sales?

9. What's your monthly tool budget for your book business?
   - How much for repricing specifically?

10. At what price would ISBNScout be:
    - A no-brainer (so cheap you'd buy immediately)?
    - Expensive but worth it (you'd have to think about it)?
    - Too expensive (wouldn't buy)?

### Competition
11. What other repricing tools have you tried?
    - What did you like about them?
    - Why did you stop using them?
    - How does ISBNScout compare?

### Product
12. If you were building ISBNScout, what would you add?
    - What features?
    - What integrations?
    - What improvements?

13. What would you remove or simplify?

## Feedback Collection Tools

### Option 1: Google Forms (Free)
- Create form with questions above
- Send link to beta testers
- Export responses to Google Sheets

### Option 2: Typeform (Free tier: 10 questions, 100 responses)
- More polished UI
- Better user experience
- Logic jumps (show Q5 only if Q4 = Yes)

### Option 3: Tally (Free, unlimited)
- Similar to Typeform
- No limits on free tier
- Good Notion integration

### Option 4: Discord/Slack Polls
- Quick async feedback
- Good for single questions
- Easy to get fast responses

### Option 5: Loom Video Feedback
- Ask testers to record 2-min Loom
- Walk through their experience
- See actual usage patterns

## What to Do With Feedback

1. **Triage bugs**
   - P0 (Blocking): Fix immediately
   - P1 (Important): Fix this week
   - P2 (Nice to fix): Backlog

2. **Categorize feature requests**
   - Must-have (mentioned by 5+ people)
   - Should-have (mentioned by 2-4 people)
   - Nice-to-have (mentioned by 1 person)

3. **Identify patterns**
   - Do all eBay sellers want X?
   - Do high-volume sellers need Y?
   - Do beginners struggle with Z?

4. **Validate pricing**
   - Calculate median "would pay"
   - Look for clustering ($29 vs $49?)
   - Check against competitor pricing

5. **Make decision**
   - Launch? Yes if 3+ would pay target price
   - Pivot? Yes if clear pattern shows different need
   - Kill? Yes if no one sees value after 4 weeks
